Turning ideas into systems.

This page is not a portfolio of experiments.

Projects

It’s a selection of real systems I have built, led, or contributed to,

in contexts where ambiguity, responsibility, and real-world consequences were present.

Current focus

  • Multiple stakeholders need alignment
  • Structure is incomplete or evolving
  • Decisions carry operational, reputational, or economic weight
  • Long-term sustainability matters more than short-term visibility

Examples of real systems

Palio of Vigevano

A large-scale historical and cultural event with strong public exposure, operating in a non-hierarchical and highly variable environment.

The system involves:

  • Public institutions
  • Sponsors and foundations
  • Dozens of volunteers and contributors
  • Thousands of participants and visitors

The context includes real logistical, organizational, and reputational risk.

My role is to:

  • Hold vision and delivery together
  • Take responsibility where formal structure is missing
  • Enable coordination across very different actors
  • Ensure continuity and reliability year after year

This is an example of leadership without formal authority, applied at real scale.

StayActive (closed project)

StayActive was a startup operating at the intersection of technology, health, and behavioral change.

The project is now closed, but it remains a formative experience.

I led the initiative end-to-end:

  • From vision and product definition
  • To execution, partnerships, and external positioning

The work required navigating:

  • Uncertainty and limited resources
  • Strategic trade-offs
  • Responsibility without guarantees

StayActive shaped how I approach system design, decision-making under pressure, and the cost of real-world constraints.

Healthcare & clinical software (ISICO)

Consulting work at the intersection of software and healthcare, supporting clinical and rehabilitation workflows.

The focus has been on:

  • Translating complex clinical needs into usable systems
  • Long-term maintainability over short-term features
  • Reliability in contexts where trust and accuracy are critical

This work reinforced a system-oriented approach in environments where errors have real consequences.

Insurance & financial systems

Ongoing involvement in insurance and financial organizations facing structural and technological transition.

The work typically involves:

  • Redesigning processes where legacy structures no longer scale
  • Introducing automation and digital tools responsibly
  • Supporting decision-making in contexts with economic exposure

The emphasis is always on making systems work, not just making them modern.

Community & digital pastoral projects

Design and support of digital initiatives within community and faith-based environments.

These systems often operate:

  • Without formal authority
  • With volunteers rather than employees
  • Under strong ethical and relational constraints

They provide a different but valuable perspective on coordination, trust, and long-term engagement.

What connects these projects

Across very different domains, the pattern is consistent:

  • Ambiguity is the norm
  • Structure must often be built while moving
  • Responsibility cannot be delegated away
  • Systems must work with real people, not ideal ones

That’s where I tend to operate best.
